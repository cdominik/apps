Physics of Aggregation

Aggregation models categorize how matter clumps together across different environments. By varying Movement (Diffusion vs. Ballistic) and Scale (Particle-Cluster vs. Cluster-Cluster), we can predict the density and fractal dimension of a material.

Hierarchical Logic: The BCCA Advantage

While standard BCCA merges clusters as they naturally encounter one another, the Hierarchical model uses a structured "mass-doubling" event. This allows us to maintain strict control over the fractal growth at each stage and is the key to simulating extremely large structures (106 particles) without crashing the browser.

Sticking Probability: DLA vs. RLA

A hidden variable in DLA is the "Sticking Probability." When probability is 100%, particles stick to the first tip they touch, creating the classic DLA fractal. When probability is low (< 5%), it becomes RLA (Reaction-Limited Aggregation). In RLA, particles bounce deep into the cluster before bonding, resulting in a much denser core.
