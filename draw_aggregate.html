Sphere Aggregate — Help

Draw chains of spheres to build a 3D aggregate. Rotate between strokes to grow arms in different directions.

Drawing

MobileOne finger or stylus draws a stroke. When you lift, the stroke becomes a chain of spheres.
DesktopLeft-click and drag to draw. Release to commit the chain.
BothIf a stroke starts or ends on an existing sphere, it connects to that sphere at its 3D depth. If both ends hit existing spheres, the chain forms a genuine 3D bridge — smoothly interpolating depth between them along the stroke.

Rotating

MobileTwo fingers rotate the structure — drag to rotate, twist for roll. Works in any mode.
DesktopRight-click and drag to rotate, anytime. (Or switch to Rotate mode and use left-click.)

Zooming

MobilePinch with two fingers.
DesktopMouse wheel — zooms around the cursor.

Modes

Draw — single finger/left-click draws.
Rotate — single finger/left-click rotates instead. Useful on a touchscreen without a stylus or when you want to carefully rotate without risk of drawing.

On desktop you can usually leave Draw mode on permanently and use right-drag for rotation.

Sliders

Size — radius of new spheres. Changes only apply to the next stroke; existing spheres keep their size.

Spread — random variation in grain size within each stroke. At 0, all new spheres are exactly the Size radius. Higher values produce a natural log-normal mix of large and small grains.

Temp — random position deviation from the drawn line. At 0, spheres sit exactly on your stroke. Higher values give a more hand-built, irregular look. Chain connectivity is preserved at all temperatures.

Depth — strength of the atmospheric depth cue (misting toward the background for far spheres). Pure visual effect, doesn't change the geometry.

Buttons

Undo — removes the most recently drawn stroke.
Clear — removes the entire aggregate (with confirmation).
Reset View — resets rotation, pan and zoom to defaults. Your aggregate is unchanged.
Colors — pick a background color and sphere color. Includes a transparent background option for exporting PNGs that overlay onto other images.
Share — opens a preview where you can rotate, zoom, and add perspective for a nicer image before exporting. Tap Share to send the PNG via the system share sheet (mobile) or download it (desktop). The preview is cosmetic only — your aggregate isn't changed.
Export — saves the aggregate as an ASCII text file. First line is the number of spheres; each following line is index radius x y z.
