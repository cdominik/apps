LEVITATION LAB

A real laboratory experiment, in playable form.

The ERC funded project GT4Pebbles, Ground Truth for Pebbles, is a program to study how planet formation begins in disks around young stars. In our lab, we levitate dust particles in a rotating drum for extended periods of time to allow them to collide and grow.

This simulation captures the physics of that experiment — the same equations, the same trade-offs — and lets you explore them yourself. Once the experiment is running in the real world, we will also expose real lab data in this game.

Particles fall through a thin gas. Spin the drum, and the gas drags them into orbits. Hold them in the highlighted zone long enough and they become levitated. You can explore yourself, or you can enter GAME mode to walk you through a series of increasingly difficult challenges to make you master of the levitation drum.

LEVITATION LAB · Manual

The drum is a cylindrical vacuum chamber viewed head-on. Particles are injected from the brass manifold above the drum and fall through rarified gas toward the bottom wall. By rotating the drum, you rotate the gas with it, which drags particles into orbits. The goal is to levitate as many particles as possible in the highlighted region on the right of the drum.

How to play

  • Set up the run with the left wing: number of particles (1–1000), settling velocity vt, optional spread of vt across particles, and the time interval over which particles are injected. Particles with larger vt appear visibly larger.
  • Inject particles with the right-wing button. You can start rotating the drum before or after injection.
  • Rotate the drum by swiping across it. To slow down, swipe in the opposite direction. If swiping does not work well on your interface, activate the Ω Ctrl button and the use the +Ω / −Ω curved arrows.
  • Particles that hit the wall become red (LOST). Particles floating are yellow. A particle that stays in the highlighted region for at least one full rotation period turns green and counts as LEVITATED.

Optional instruments

  • Sound — motor hum (pitch tracks rotation speed), a dull plonk when a particle is lost, and a soft chime when one becomes levitated.
  • Trails — each particle leaves a fading trail of its recent path, so orbits become visible. Disabled at very high particle counts.
  • Laser — engages lidar mode: the drum interior goes dark and particles become invisible. A scanning green laser beam sweeps the chamber, briefly revealing any particles it crosses.
  • Lighting — toggles the lab between a dim night setting and a bright day setting.
  • Microscope / Chart — slide the ceiling-mounted viewport down to display close-up imagery (microscope) or live data plots (chart). What is shown depends on the current state of the experiment, driven by an external rules file.

Game & Challenge

  • Game Mode — Progress through predefined levels with locked parameters. You must levitate a target number of particles for a set duration to clear the level.
  • Challenge Mode — A high-score competition. Using the current parameter setup, levitate as many particles as you can for 3 full orbits, then enter your name on the local leaderboard.
