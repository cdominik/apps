LEVITATION LAB · Manual

The drum is a cylindrical gas chamber viewed head-on. Particles are injected from the brass manifold above the drum and fall through nitrogen gas toward the bottom wall. By rotating the drum, you rotate the gas with it, which drags particles into orbits. The goal is to levitate as many particles as possible in the highlighted region on the right of the drum.

How to play

  • Set up the run with the left wing: number of particles (1–100), settling velocity vt, optional spread of vt across particles, and the time interval over which particles are injected. Particles with larger vt appear visibly larger.
  • Inject particles with the right-wing button. You can start rotating the drum before or after injection.
  • Rotate the drum by swiping across it, or by clicking the +Ω / −Ω curved arrows on the left side of the drum (enable them with the Ω Ctrl button first). To slow the drum down, swipe in the opposite direction of its spin.
  • Particles that hit the wall become red (LOST). Particles floating are yellow. A particle that stays in the highlighted region for at least one full rotation period turns green and counts as LEVITATED.

Optional instruments

  • Laser — a scanning green beam sweeps the drum; particles briefly flash when illuminated.
  • Gas Flow — shows subtle streamlines of the rotating gas inside the drum.
  • Sound — motor hum (pitch tracks rotation speed), a dull plonk when a particle is lost, and a soft chime when one becomes levitated.
  • Theme — switch between dark (night lab) and light (day lab) ambient lighting.

Press Esc or × to close.
