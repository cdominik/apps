Visibility is computed from low-precision Keplerian orbital elements (VSOP-style approximations) and the planet's altitude above the horizon at civil twilight (sun 6° below horizon). A planet is considered visible if it is high enough for its apparent magnitude to overcome twilight and airmass extinction. Evening = after sunset; morning = before sunrise. Event markers (outlined cells) flag conjunctions within 3° and oppositions / greatest elongations. For exact rise/set times at your location, use a planetarium app such as Stellarium. Calculations give a reliable qualitative picture but may differ from published almanacs by a day or two at apparition edges.
