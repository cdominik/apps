Visibility is computed from low-precision Keplerian orbital elements (Schlyter/Meeus) and the planet's altitude at civil twilight (sun 6° below horizon). A planet is considered visible if it is high enough for its apparent magnitude to overcome twilight brightness. Evening = after sunset; morning = before sunrise. Outlined cells mark days with a notable event (conjunction, opposition, greatest elongation). Accuracy is sufficient for categorising visibility — for precise rise/set times use a planetarium app such as Stellarium.
